# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Technically curious people evaluating memory for LLMs, especially developers and agent builders who want to understand what a durable memory system actually gives an agent. They arrive skeptical, often late at night, looking for something concrete instead of another abstract AI pitch.

## Product Purpose
grug-brain gives an agent a local-first memory system built from markdown, search, organization primitives, and git-backed history. It exists to make memory operational: searchable, inspectable, durable, and synchronized across machines without turning the agent into a black box.

## Brand Personality
Calm, confident, technical. The voice should feel precise, grounded, and a little archival: less startup theater, more infrastructure with taste.

## Anti-references
Generic SaaS heroes with repeated feature cards, glowing AI gradients, fake dashboards used as filler, and copy that promises intelligence without showing mechanics. The page should not feel like a glossy wrapper around vague claims.

## Design Principles
- Treat memory as infrastructure, not magic.
- Show operational mechanics before making big claims.
- Use depth and motion to express history and persistence.
- Make the system feel local, durable, and inspectable.
- Keep the tone controlled, technical, and readable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-accessible navigation. Respect reduced-motion preferences, avoid hover-only meaning, and keep long-form copy readable on both mobile and large screens.
